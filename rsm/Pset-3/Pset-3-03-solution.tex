Expand the expression:
\begin{align*}
x^{2} - 3 y^{2} - 12y + 14 = 0
\end{align*}

Solve for $y$ in terms of $x$, assuming $x\in(-\infty, -\sqrt{2}) \cup (+\sqrt{2}, \infty)$:
\begin{align*}
y = \frac{1}{3} \bigl(- \sqrt{3} \sqrt{x^{2}-2} - 6 \bigr)
\end{align*}

Real solutions:
\begin{align*}
x & = -\sqrt{2}, y=-2
\\
x & = +\sqrt{2}, y=-2
\end{align*}
