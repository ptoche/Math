Solve for $y$ in terms of $x$. There are two solutions:
\begin{align*}
y & = - \frac{x^{2}-1}{\sqrt{2}}
\\
y & = \frac{x^{2}-1}{\sqrt{2}}
\end{align*}

The solutions of Pell's equation are:
\begin{align*}
x & = \pm \frac{1}{2}(-(3-2\sqrt{2}))^{n} - (3+2\sqrt{2})^{n})
\\
y & = \pm \frac{(3-2\sqrt{2})^{n}-(3+2\sqrt{2})^{n}}{2\sqrt{2}}
\end{align*}
for $n\in\mathbb{Z}$, $n\ge0$.
